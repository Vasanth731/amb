Physical Sciences

The position, velocity and acceleration of an object in motion can be written as functions of time.

\[ s(t) = 5t^{2} + 2t + 1 \] \[ v(t) = 10t + 2 \] \[ a(t) = 10 \]

Velocity is the derivative of displacement and acceleration is the derivative of velocity.

\[ \frac{ds}{dt} = v \] \[ \frac{dv}{dt} = \frac{d^{2}s}{dt^{2}} = a \]

Newton’s first law states that an object remains in constant motion unless acted upon by an external force.

\[ p = mv \] \[ \frac{dp}{dt} = m\frac{dv}{dt} \]

If acceleration is constant then the change in velocity is zero and therefore the force is zero, which explains the law of inertia.

\[ \frac{dv}{dt} = 0 \] \[ F = 0 \]

The second law is the law of acceleration, relating force, mass and acceleration.

\[ p = mv \] \[ \frac{dp}{dt} = m\frac{dv}{dt} \] \[ a = \frac{F}{m} \]

The third law states that every action has an equal and opposite reaction.

\[ p_{AB} + p_{BA} = m_A v_A + m_B v_B \] \[ \frac{dp_{AB}}{dt} + \frac{dp_{BA}}{dt} = m_A \frac{dv_A}{dt} + m_B \frac{dv_B}{dt} \] \[ F_{AB} = -F_{BA} \]

The equations of motion can be derived from acceleration.

\[ a = \frac{dv}{dt} \] \[ \int_{v_0}^{v} dv = \int a\,dt \] \[ v - v_0 = at \] \[ v = v_0 + at \]

Velocity is also the derivative of displacement.

\[ v = \frac{ds}{dt} \] \[ v_0 + at = \frac{ds}{dt} \] \[ \int v_0 dt + \int at\,dt = \int ds \] \[ s = v_0 t + \frac{at^2}{2} \]

Another equation of motion can be derived using the chain rule.

\[ a = \frac{dv}{dt} = \frac{dv}{ds}\frac{ds}{dt} \] \[ a = v\frac{dv}{ds} \] \[ \int a\,ds = \int v\,dv \] \[ as = \frac{v^2}{2} - \frac{v_0^2}{2} \] \[ v_0^2 + 2as = v^2 \]

These ideas extend to rotational mechanics and to the concepts of work, energy and power.

\[ W = \int_A^B F \cdot ds \] \[ P = F \times V \] \[ E_{KE} = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 \] \[ E_{PE} = mgh \]

The kinetic theory of gases explains the relationship between pressure, volume and temperature.

\[ PV = nRT \]

Fluid dynamics studies the behavior of fluids and pressure.

\[ P = \frac{F}{A} \] \[ P_1A_1V_1 = P_2A_2V_2 \]

Waves and oscillations describe periodic motion.

\[ p(x,t) = A \sin(kx - \omega t + \phi) \]

Optics studies the behavior of light.

\[ \frac{1}{u} + \frac{1}{v} = \frac{1}{f} \]

Gravitation explains the attraction between masses.

\[ F = \frac{G M m}{r^2} \]

Thermodynamics deals with heat, work and internal energy.

\[ dQ = dU + dW \]

Here \(dQ\) is heat, \(dU\) is internal energy and \(dW\) is the work done by the system.

Electromagnetism is described by Maxwell's equations which explain electric and magnetic fields and the nature of light.

\[ \nabla \cdot \mathbf{B} = 0 \] \[ \nabla \cdot \mathbf{D} = \rho_v \] \[ \nabla \times \mathbf{E} = -\frac{\partial \mathbf{B}}{\partial t} \] \[ \nabla \times \mathbf{H} = \mathbf{J} + \frac{\partial \mathbf{D}}{\partial t} \]

Modern physics explores the quantum realm where matter is made of fundamental particles.

\[ u, c, t \qquad \bar{u}, \bar{c}, \bar{t} \] \[ d, s, b \qquad \bar{d}, \bar{s}, \bar{b} \]

These are quarks and their antiparticles.

\[ e^{-}, \mu^{-}, \tau^{-} \qquad e^{+}, \mu^{+}, \tau^{+} \] \[ \nu_e, \nu_\mu, \nu_\tau \qquad \bar{\nu}_e, \bar{\nu}_\mu, \bar{\nu}_\tau \]

These are leptons and their antiparticles.

\[ \gamma, W^{+}, W^{-}, Z^{0}, g, H^{0} \]

These particles are known as bosons, the force carriers of the universe.

Protons and neutrons form the nucleus and electrons orbit around them, giving rise to all forms of matter in the universe.
