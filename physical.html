Physical Sciences

Physical sciences study non-living systems and the fundamental laws governing matter and energy. Physics explores the basic principles of the universe from quantum mechanics to classical mechanics, while chemistry examines the composition, structure, and transformations of matter.

From the smallest subatomic particles to the forces that bind atoms into molecules, physical sciences reveal the underlying order and beauty of nature. They provide the foundation for technology, engineering, and our understanding of how the physical universe operates.
