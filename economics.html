Economics

Economics studies how individuals, businesses, governments, and nations make choices about allocating scarce resources. It examines production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services, and the systems through which societies organize economic activity.

Microeconomics focuses on individual decision-making and market mechanisms, while macroeconomics examines economy-wide phenomena like inflation, unemployment, and economic growth. Economics helps us understand incentives, trade-offs, and the forces shaping prosperity and inequality.
