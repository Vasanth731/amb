Life Sciences

Molecules combine to form compounds, and within living organisms, genetic information stored in DNA is transcribed into RNA and then translated into proteins, forming the molecular basis of all life. The diversity of life is broadly classified into five kingdoms, each with its own subdivisions. The Monera kingdom includes simple prokaryotic organisms such as Archaebacteria and Eubacteria. Protista consists of mostly unicellular eukaryotes, including algae, protozoa, and fungus-like protists. The Fungi kingdom comprises organisms like Zygomycota, Ascomycota, Basidiomycota, and Deuteromycota, which play key roles in decomposition. Plantae includes a wide range of plant life, from simpler groups like Thallophyta, Bryophyta, and Pteridophyta to more complex seed-bearing plants grouped under Phanerogamae, which include Gymnosperms and Angiosperms. Finally, the Animalia kingdom encompasses diverse multicellular organisms, classified into groups such as Porifera, Coelenterata, Platyhelminthes, Nematoda, Annelida, Arthropoda, Mollusca, Echinodermata, and Chordata, representing increasing levels of complexity in structure and function..
