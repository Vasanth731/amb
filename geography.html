Geography

Landforms

Mountains are very high landforms with steep slopes and peaks. Hills are smaller, rounded elevations of land. Plateaus are flat-topped highlands raised above the surrounding land. Plains are large, flat or gently rolling lowlands. Valleys are low areas between hills or mountains, often formed by rivers. Deserts are dry regions that receive very little rainfall. Dunes are mounds of sand shaped by the wind, commonly found in deserts. Canyons are deep valleys with steep sides, usually carved by rivers over time. Cliffs are steep or nearly vertical rock faces. Volcanoes are openings in the Earth’s crust through which lava, ash, and gases erupt.

Water Bodies

Oceans are vast bodies of salt water that cover most of the Earth’s surface. Seas are smaller parts of oceans, often partially enclosed by land. Rivers are flowing bodies of freshwater that move toward seas, lakes, or oceans. Lakes are large inland bodies of standing water. Ponds are smaller and shallower bodies of still water. Streams are narrow, flowing water bodies, often smaller than rivers. Waterfalls are places where water flows over a steep drop or cliff. Glaciers are large masses of ice that move slowly over land. Ice caps are thick layers of ice that cover extensive land areas, especially in polar regions.

Coastal and Ocean Features

Beaches are sandy or pebbly shores found along the edges of water bodies. Bays are curved inlets of water that are partially enclosed by land. Gulfs are large and deep bays with narrow openings to the sea. Straits are narrow passages of water that connect two larger water bodies. An isthmus is a narrow strip of land that connects two larger land areas. Peninsulas are landforms surrounded by water on three sides. Capes are points of land that extend outward into a body of water. Fjords are long, deep inlets of the sea with steep sides, typically formed by glaciers. Lagoons are shallow bodies of water separated from the sea by sandbars, coral reefs, or barrier islands.

Other Natural Features

Forests are large areas of land densely covered with trees. Wetlands are regions where the land is saturated with water, forming a transition between land and water. Marshes are wetlands dominated by grasses and soft plants. Swamps are wetlands characterized by the presence of trees and woody vegetation. Grasslands are open regions covered mainly with grasses and very few trees. Tundra is a cold, treeless region where the ground remains frozen for most of the year.

Based on the land features and the settlement of people the political countries emerged in the continents of Africa, Europe, Middle-East, Asia, Australia, Antractica, North and South America
